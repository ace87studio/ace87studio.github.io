About

What is it?

Sealcoating Simulator is a first-person game about a job most people never think about: sealing asphalt. You grab the hose, pour the sealant, and drag it around with the squeegee until the last patch of grey concrete is gone. Keep an eye on your tank, refill when you run low, and aim for a clean 100%. When the lot is done, slide between the before and after and take in the difference. The twist is the sealant itself. It actually flows, pools and settles like a real liquid, so every job feels a little different.

Highlights

Features

💧

Sealant that actually flows

Pour it and it really moves. It spreads, pools, levels out and slowly settles like a real liquid. No two driveways ever end up looking the same.

🧴

The hose

Pour material with different spouts, each with its own spread and colour. Your tank drains while you work, so you cannot just spray forever.

🧹

The squeegee

Spread the sealant in any direction, switch the head between layouts, and push the coat just by walking across it.

⛽

Tank and refill loop

Out of material? Find a refill station. They light up when you look at them and pulse through walls when your tank runs dry, so you never wander around lost.

✅

Coverage that counts

Every surface knows exactly how covered it is. Missed spots glow so you can track them down, and the level wraps up at a full 100%.

🪟

The before and after

Each level takes a photo before you start and after you finish. The win screen lets you drag a slider between the two to show off the work.

⏱️

Times, stars and payouts

Beat your own best time (pausing does not count), earn up to three stars, and pocket each level's reward.

🗺️

Levels and modes

Pick jobs from a level hub that remembers your best runs, across Free, Story and Challenge play.

🎨

A warm toon world

A cosy cream and orange cartoon style, a glossy wet-look sealant, and a friendly suburban block to work your way through.

Why it stands out

Easy to love, hard to put down

A simple, oddly satisfying idea taken seriously: clean, glossy, and strangely relaxing, the kind of game you start for five minutes and look up an hour later.

  • Oddly satisfying by design. Pouring, spreading and watching grey turn glossy black just feels good.
  • Sealant that flows for real, so every job plays out a little differently.
  • Pick-up-and-play. Pour, spread, hit a clean 100% and slide between the before and after.
  • Made for clips and streams. A warm toon look and a glossy finish that pop on screen.
  • Bite-sized levels with times, stars and rewards to chase a cleaner, faster run.
  • Cozy, low-stress vibe. No fail states, no timers breathing down your neck. Just the work.
